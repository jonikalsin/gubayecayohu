Are Cristiano Ronaldo's twins adopted?

Cristiano Ronaldo earned his first Ballon d’Or at 23years of age.

The five-time Ballon d’Or medalist, who was born and bred in Madeira, began his senior club career with Sporting CP before joining Manchester United at the age of 18 in 2003, winning the FA Cup in his first season.

He went on to win three Premier League championships in a row, the Champions League, and the FIFA Club World Cup.

Ronaldo has never married, although he has had a long-term relationship with model Georgina Rodrguez, whom he met while she was working in a boutique. He doesn’t have a wife.

They recently announced that they were expecting twins, with Ronaldo announcing the news on Instagram.

Ronaldo disclosed earlier today that one of the twins had died.

Are Cristiano Ronaldo’s twins adopted?

Cristiano Ronaldo did not adopt his twins, instead, he became their father through surrogacy.

CR7 announced that he had become a father to two twins via surrogacy shortly after Portugal’s loss to Chile in the semi-finals of the 2017 Confederations Cup.

Given that Cristiano was dating Georgina Rodriguez at the time, the announcement surprised many fans.

Cristiano Ronaldo did not give any rationale as to why he picked a surrogate mother, he was simply delighted that he now had twins.
