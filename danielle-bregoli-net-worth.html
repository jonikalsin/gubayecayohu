Early Life of Danielle Bregoli

Danielle Peskowitz Bregoli was born on March 23, 2003, in Boynton Beach, Florida. Her parents, Barbara Ann Bregoli and Ira Peskowitz, had been dating for a year when Barbara became pregnant. Her mother is of Italian descent, and her father is Jewish. She was primarily raised by her mother and was brought up as a Catholic. Her father is a deputy with the Palm Beach County Sheriff’s Office, but she is estranged from him.

Danielle Bregoli’s Rise to Fame and Fortune

Danielle Bregoli became famous after appearing on the television series “Dr. Phil” in 2016. She quickly gained a social media following and appeared in the music video “Everything 1K’ by Kodak Black. Bregoli released her debut single “These Heaux” in 2017 and became the youngest female rapper ever to appear on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. She signed a record deal with Atlantic Records and has a reality show, a makeup brand, tours, and a music career.

Bregoli has made the most of her fame, cashing in on every opportunity that comes her way. As of this writing, she has 17 million followers on Instagram, 568,000 followers on Twitter, and 7.4 million subscribers on YouTube. She gets paid significant sums of money to do paid posts and product placements on Instagram. Danielle Bregoli can easily make up to $300,000 a month on product ads, and as much as $100,000 for a single post.

On July 11, 2019, it was revealed that Danielle had signed a $1 million songwriting/publishing deal with a company called Pulse Music Group. The deal pays Danielle $350,000 upfront and then will pay two additional $350,000 installments after reaching set revenue milestones. She will also earn 70% of future record sales profits.

In addition to product placements, Bregoli is also reportedly charging up to $40,000 for meet and greet sessions. She ultimately earned around $1 million from her own 25-city tour.

In December 2018, Danielle signed a deal with Snapchat to create a reality series for the social network. The Snapchat show, “Bringing Up Bhabie,” had 10 million unique viewers in the first 24 hours. The series has 12 episodes.

In January 2019, Danielle earned $900,000 to endorse a new cosmetics company called CopyCat Beauty for six months. She will also earn a cut of the company’s profits. If the endorsement performs well, Danielle’s contract entitles her to a large bonus and the opportunity to create her own makeup line.

It was reported that Bregoli is going to act in movies and on television.

Danielle Bregoli Earns $50 Million in One Year from OnlyFans

Danielle Bregoli, also known as Bhad Bhabie, made headlines in April 2021 when she joined subscription platform OnlyFans at the age of 18 and reportedly earned $1 million in just six hours. She went on to claim in August 2021 that her OnlyFans income alone made her rich enough to pay $4 million cash for a mansion and retire forever.

However, many were skeptical when she announced in April 2022 that she had already made $50 million from OnlyFans after being on the platform for just one year. Bregoli then posted an earnings statement screenshot to her Instagram account, which appears to be real, showing that she had grossed nearly $53 million from OnlyFans between April 1, 2021 and April 25, 2022. After the platform’s cut, she netted $42 million in pre-tax net income.

According to the earnings statement, Bregoli earned $16 million from subscriptions, $25 million from messages, and $160,000 from tips. Her success on OnlyFans has allowed her to purchase a $6 million mansion outside of Boca Raton.
